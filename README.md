### NHL Hockey Hacks: Predictive xG Modeling & Data Pipeline

This project is a high-performance Expected Goals(xG) engine designed to transform raw NHL play-by-play data into actionable predictive insights. I am starting to build the infrastructure with an ETL pipeline that ingests, cleans, and structures over 250,000 individual shot records into a PostgreSQL database. By normalizing spatial coordinates and engineering features like shot angle, distance, and situational context (like rebounds and shot attempts on the rush in a counterattacking situation), the system establishes a "physics baseline" for what constitutes a high-danger scoring chance in the modern NHL, which will hopefully become more defined as more advanced datapoints come out as a result of 'NHL EDGE'.

On the machine learning side, I implemented a LightGBM classifier specifically tuned for tabular sports data. To ensure the model is ready for real-world betting or performance analysis, I used a strict three-way temporal split—training on historical seasons and testing only on the most recent "held-out" season. This prevents the common pitfall of data leakage and ensures the resulting 0.77 AUC is a true reflection of the model's ability to predict future outcomes. I also put in reliability diagrams to confirm that the model's probabilities are perfectly calibrated with actual league goal rates.

The future of this project involves moving from league-wide averages to individual player identities. I am currently developing a "v2" layer that will calculate player-specific finishing residuals and goalie suppression metrics to identify who is truly elite versus who is simply benefiting from high-danger volume. Ultimately, this will power a live web dashboard that predicts player props, such as the specific percentage chance of a player hitting their "Shots on Goal" target or a goalie’s expected save percentage for any given nightly matchup.

I want to eventually make a fullscale website to the effect of what 'ballparkpal.com' has. With statistics that help not only sports bettors but anyone who wants to learn further about data the analytics of hockey.
